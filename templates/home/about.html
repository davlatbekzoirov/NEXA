Why NEXA exists

Most students juggle their academics in one app, their budget in a notes file, their club hours in memory, and their college or grad-school applications in a spreadsheet that's always slightly out of date. NEXA started as four separate tools built to solve each of those problems properly — and grew into one connected system, because the problems were never really separate.

How we build

Every module is designed around the same idea: track the real thing, not a simplified version of it. That means a debt simplifier that actually minimizes transactions, a grade calculator that runs What-If and Target Grade scenarios, and a deadline engine that emails you before it's too late — not a generic to-do list wearing a school-themed skin.

What NEXA covers

four modules, one dashboard
UniTracker

Applications, deadlines & documents

Every school you're applying to — admissions deadlines, required documents, scholarship windows — tracked in one list instead of scattered across emails and a spreadsheet that's always a version behind.

  • Per-school deadline countdowns, sorted by urgency
  • Document checklist so nothing's missing at submission
  • Scholarship deadlines tracked alongside admissions ones
  • Email reminders while there's still time to act
SmartStudy

Grades & study scheduling

Know exactly what a final needs to be for the grade you want, and get a week-by-week study plan built from your actual syllabus — not a generic planner template.

  • What-If scenarios: model any grade before it happens
  • Target Grade calculator: minimum score needed to hit a goal
  • Syllabus-based weekly study schedule
  • Per-course grade breakdown, always current
CampusPulse

Volunteer hours & leadership

Log hours, roles, and organizations as they happen, so the record already exists the week a resume, scholarship application, or grad school form asks for it.

  • Hour logging by organization and role
  • Leadership positions tracked over time
  • Exportable history for applications and resumes
  • No more reconstructing activities from memory
CampusCribs

Shared bills & spending runway

A debt simplifier that minimizes who-owes-who transactions between roommates, plus a running read on how long your money lasts at your current pace.

  • Shared expense splitting across roommates
  • Transaction-minimizing settle-up algorithm
  • Spending runway: days left at current burn rate
  • One view instead of a shared notes app
